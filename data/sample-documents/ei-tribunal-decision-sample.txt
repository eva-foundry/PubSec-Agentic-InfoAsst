OVERVIEW

[1] The Claimant appeals the decision of the General Division dated October 15,
2025 (GD-25-0893). The General Division had dismissed the Claimant's appeal from
the Commission's decision that the Claimant had not accumulated sufficient hours of
insurable employment to qualify for Employment Insurance ("EI") regular benefits
under section 7 of the Employment Insurance Act, S.C. 1996, c. 23 (the "Act").

[2] The Claimant argues that the General Division erred in law by failing to consider
whether certain hours worked during a period of reduced work schedule constituted
insurable employment within the meaning of subsection 7(2) of the Act, read in
conjunction with sections 9.1 and 10 of the Employment Insurance Regulations,
SOR/96-332 (the "Regulations").

[3] For the reasons set out below, I find that the General Division did err in law in its
interpretation and application of the qualifying period provisions. The appeal is
allowed.


ISSUES

[4] The issues before me are:

    (a) Did the General Division err in law by failing to consider whether hours
    worked during a reduced work schedule constituted insurable hours under
    section 7 of the Act and section 9.1 of the Regulations?

    (b) Did the General Division err in law by misapplying the qualifying period
    calculation under subsection 7(2) of the Act?

    (c) If the General Division erred, what is the appropriate remedy?


RELEVANT LEGISLATIVE PROVISIONS

Employment Insurance Act, S.C. 1996, c. 23

Qualification for benefits

7. (1) Unemployment benefits are payable as provided in this Part to an insured
person who qualifies to receive them.

Qualification requirements

(2) An insured person qualifies if the person

    (a) has had an interruption of earnings from employment; and

    (b) has had, during the qualifying period, the number of hours of insurable
    employment set out in the following table in relation to the regional rate of
    unemployment that applies to the person:

    Regional Rate of Unemployment      Required Number of Hours
    6% and under                        700
    more than 6% but not more than 7%   665
    more than 7% but not more than 8%   630
    more than 8% but not more than 9%   595
    more than 9% but not more than 10%  560
    more than 10% but not more than 11% 525
    more than 11% but not more than 12% 490
    more than 12% but not more than 13% 455
    more than 13%                       420

Qualifying period

(3) The qualifying period of an insured person is the shorter of

    (a) the 52-week period immediately before the beginning of a benefit period, and

    (b) the period that begins on the first day of an immediately preceding benefit
    period and ends with the end of the week before the beginning of a benefit
    period under this subsection.


Employment Insurance Regulations, SOR/96-332

Hours of insurable employment

9.1 Where a person's earnings are paid on an hourly basis, the person is considered
to have worked in insurable employment for the number of hours that the person
actually worked and for which the person was remunerated.


ANALYSIS

Issue (a): Hours during reduced work schedule

[5] The Claimant worked as a seasonal employee at a fish processing plant in
Shediac, New Brunswick, from May 2025 to September 2025. During the months of
August and September 2025, the plant reduced operations from a five-day to a
three-day work week due to supply shortages.

[6] The Commission determined that the Claimant accumulated 612 hours of
insurable employment during the qualifying period. The regional rate of unemployment
for the Claimant's economic region (Moncton) was 8.3% at the relevant time,
requiring 630 hours under subsection 7(2)(b) of the Act.

[7] The General Division accepted the Commission's calculation without examining
whether additional hours worked by the Claimant during the reduced schedule period
had been properly counted. The Claimant submitted pay stubs showing 48 additional
hours worked during August and September 2025 that were paid at a different rate
classification by the employer.

[8] Section 9.1 of the Regulations provides that where earnings are paid on an hourly
basis, a person is considered to have worked in insurable employment for the number
of hours actually worked and for which the person was remunerated. The Claimant's
pay stubs clearly show remuneration for these 48 hours, albeit under a different pay
code used by the employer for "standby" or "reduced schedule" hours.

[9] I find that the General Division erred in law by failing to consider the application
of section 9.1 of the Regulations to these disputed hours. The provision is clear: if the
hours were actually worked and the person was remunerated for them, they constitute
insurable hours regardless of the pay classification used by the employer. See
Canada (Attorney General) v. Laverdiere, 2017 FCA 203, at paragraphs 22-25, where
the Federal Court of Appeal confirmed that the character of hours for EI purposes
depends on the substance of the employment relationship, not the employer's
internal classification.

[10] This principle was further affirmed in Commission v. Belanger, SST-AD-24-0315,
where the Appeal Division held that hours worked under a modified schedule
arrangement remained insurable where the claimant performed productive work and
received compensation.

Issue (b): Qualifying period calculation

[11] Having found that the 48 disputed hours should have been included, the
Claimant's total insurable hours during the qualifying period amount to 660 hours
(612 + 48). The required threshold for the Moncton economic region at the relevant
time was 630 hours. The Claimant therefore meets the qualification requirement
under subsection 7(2)(b) of the Act.

[12] I note that the General Division also did not address the Claimant's alternative
argument regarding the extension of the qualifying period under subsection 7(4) of
the Act due to a period of illness in June 2025. Given my finding on the primary issue,
it is not necessary to address this alternative argument.

Issue (c): Remedy

[13] Pursuant to subsection 59(1) of the Department of Employment and Social
Development Act, S.C. 2005, c. 34, the Appeal Division may give the decision that
the General Division should have given or refer the matter back to the General
Division. Given that the factual record is complete and there is no need for further
findings of fact, I find it appropriate to give the decision that the General Division
should have given.


DECISION

[14] The appeal is allowed. The Commission's decision that the Claimant did not
qualify for EI regular benefits is rescinded.

[15] The Claimant accumulated 660 hours of insurable employment during the
qualifying period, which exceeds the required 630 hours for the applicable economic
region. The Claimant qualifies for EI regular benefits under section 7 of the Act.

[16] The matter is returned to the Commission for the calculation and payment of
benefits in accordance with the Act.
