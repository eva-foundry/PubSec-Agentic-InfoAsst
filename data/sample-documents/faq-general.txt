EVA DOMAIN ASSISTANT - FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
===================================================


Q1: What is the EVA Domain Assistant?

A: EVA Domain Assistant (EVA DA) is an AI-powered retrieval-augmented generation
(RAG) platform operated by the AI Centre of Expertise (AICoE) at Employment and
Social Development Canada (ESDC). It enables authorized users to search, query,
and analyze documents within managed workspaces using large language models
combined with enterprise search capabilities.


Q2: What types of documents can I upload?

A: EVA DA supports the following file formats: PDF (.pdf), Microsoft Word (.docx),
plain text (.txt), Markdown (.md), PowerPoint (.pptx), Excel (.xlsx), CSV (.csv),
HTML (.html), and images (.png, .jpg) with OCR extraction. Maximum file size is
100 MB per document. Password-protected or encrypted files must be decrypted
before upload.


Q3: What are workspace types and how do they differ?

A: EVA DA offers five workspace types:
  - Legislation: Optimized for legislative text with hierarchical chunking, section
    cross-references, and bilingual (EN/FR) support.
  - Case Law: Designed for tribunal decisions and jurisprudence with citation graphs,
    court hierarchy ranking, and case-claim mapping.
  - Knowledge Management: General-purpose document search with Q&A, summarization,
    and topic modelling.
  - FAQ: Lightweight Q&A workspace with basic analytics and export tools.
  - Sandbox: Training and experimentation workspace with all features enabled and
    no SLA guarantees.


Q4: How do I request a workspace?

A: Navigate to the Workspace Catalog in the self-service portal, select the workspace
type that matches your use case, and complete the booking wizard. You will need to
specify your desired start and end dates, complete an entry survey describing your
use case, and confirm the booking. An AICoE administrator will review and approve
your request.


Q5: What is the entry survey and why is it required?

A: The entry survey captures essential information about your planned use case,
including expected number of users, data volume, data classification level, and
business justification. This information helps the AICoE provision the appropriate
resources and ensure compliance with ESDC security and privacy requirements.


Q6: What data classification levels are supported?

A: EVA DA supports three data classification levels aligned with the Government of
Canada security classification framework:
  - Unclassified: Information intended for public release or with no security impact.
  - Protected A: Information that could cause injury to private interests if disclosed.
  - Protected B: Information that could cause serious injury to private interests if
    disclosed (e.g., personal information, financial records).
All data is processed within Canada Central / Canada East Azure regions.


Q7: What is RBAC and what roles are available?

A: Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) determines what actions each team member can
perform within a workspace. Three roles are available:
  - Reader: Can search and query documents but cannot upload or manage content.
  - Contributor: Can upload documents, manage content, and use all search features.
  - Admin: Full control including team management, configuration, and document
    lifecycle management.


Q8: How do I add team members to my workspace?

A: Workspace administrators can add team members through the Team Management page.
Navigate to your booking, select "Manage Team," and invite members by their
government email address. Assign the appropriate role (Reader, Contributor, or Admin)
based on their needs.


Q9: How is the cost of a workspace calculated?

A: Workspace costs are calculated on a weekly basis, derived from the monthly rate
for each workspace type. The total cost is computed as:
  Total Cost = (Monthly Rate / 4) x Number of Weeks
Cost recovery is managed through the FinOps framework with chargeback to the
requesting cost centre.


Q10: What happens when my booking ends?

A: When your booking reaches its end date, you will be prompted to complete an exit
survey. You can choose to:
  - Keep: Data and index are preserved for potential future use.
  - Archive: Data is moved to cold storage and index is deactivated.
  - Delete: All data, documents, and index entries are permanently removed.


Q11: Can I extend my booking?

A: Yes. Before your booking expires, you can request an extension through the
self-service portal by updating the end date. Extensions are subject to workspace
availability and require administrator approval.


Q12: What languages does EVA DA support?

A: EVA DA fully supports bilingual operation in English and French. All system
prompts are language-parameterized, and a built-in translation tool handles
cross-language queries. You can search English documents using French queries and
vice versa.


Q13: How does the citation system work?

A: Every answer generated by EVA DA includes pinpoint citations referencing the
specific source documents and sections used. Citations include the document name,
page or section number, and a relevance score. Users can click citations to view the
original source text for verification.


Q14: What is the confidence score?

A: Each answer includes a confidence score between 0.0 and 1.0 indicating the
system's assessment of answer quality. Scores above 0.8 indicate high confidence
with strong source support. Scores between 0.5 and 0.8 suggest moderate confidence.
Scores below 0.5 indicate low confidence, and the system may recommend consulting
a subject matter expert.


Q15: How do I provide feedback on answers?

A: After receiving an answer, use the thumbs up or thumbs down buttons. For rejected
answers, you can provide a correction and select a reason (e.g., incorrect citation,
incomplete answer, outdated information). Feedback is used to monitor quality and
identify content gaps.


Q16: What should I do if EVA DA cannot answer my question?

A: If EVA DA cannot find relevant sources or provides a low-confidence answer, it
will recommend escalation to a subject matter expert. You can also try rephrasing
your question, checking that relevant documents have been uploaded, or narrowing
the scope of your query.


Q17: Is my data secure?

A: All data is processed and stored within Government of Canada approved Azure
regions (Canada Central / Canada East) with no public IP exposure. Access requires
Entra ID (Azure AD) single sign-on. Data is encrypted at rest and in transit (TLS 1.3).
All operations are logged for audit purposes with no PII in log entries.


Q18: What AI models does EVA DA use?

A: EVA DA uses Azure OpenAI models managed through the AI Foundry platform.
The default model for generation is GPT-4o. Embedding models include
text-embedding-ada-002 and text-embedding-3-large. Model selection and parameters
are configured by AICoE administrators through the model registry.


Q19: Can I access EVA DA from outside the ESDC network?

A: EVA DA is accessible only through the ESDC Secure Cloud-Enabled Desktop (SCED)
or approved VPN connections. All traffic routes through Azure APIM with Entra ID
authentication. Direct public internet access is not available.


Q20: Who do I contact for support?

A: For technical issues or questions about EVA DA, contact the AI Centre of Expertise
at aicoe-ceai@esdc-edsc.gc.ca. For workspace-specific questions, contact your
workspace administrator. For urgent issues during business hours, use the EVA
support channel in Microsoft Teams.
